date: 2026-05-01

Long before backpacking became a recreational hobby, it was a necessity of survival. From indigenous hunters to early explorers, carrying only what was essential wasn't a "style"—it was a requirement for distance and endurance.

Today, the "Ultralight" movement often gets bogged down in specific gram-counts and titanium gear. But for the essentialist, it’s about a deeper philosophy: carrying exactly what you need to be safe and present in the wild, and nothing more. Whether your base weight is 10 pounds or 20, the conundrum isn't the number—it's the intent.
