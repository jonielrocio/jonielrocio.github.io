By: Joniel Rocio

A hiker from Massachusetts was found deceased Tuesday night in Lincoln, New Hampshire, after being reported overdue from  a weekend trip to Franconia Notch. The 61-year-old man had set out Saturday morning under clear skies, but was caught in a sudden storm that dropped several inches of snow in Franconia Notch area. 

![](/images/blog/lin.jpg)

New Hampshire Fish and game conservation officers, alongside PEMI Valley Search and Rescue and an Army National Guard helicopter team, located the hiker at 7:41 p.m. on a remote section of the Kinsman Pond Trail. He was approximately 5.5 miles from his vehicle.

Authorities reminded the public that while it may feel like spring at lower elevations, winter conditions—including deep snow and freezing temperatures—remain a reality in the high peaks. For the full official report and recovery details, visit the [New Hampshire Fish and Game Newsroom](https://nhfishgame.com/2026/04/22/missing-hiker-located-deceased-in-lincoln/)."
